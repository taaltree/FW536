What's in this site

Pick your theme

Each session offers two assessed problem sets — same statistical content, different ecological themes (e.g., forest vs. aquatic). Choose whichever fits your interests; the practice problems on the lab page show you what to expect.

Three problem-set variants per session

A practice problem set (answers visible) for in-class work, plus two assessed problem sets (answers in separate instructor-only KEY files) for graded submission.

Plain-language summaries

Each day has a non-mathematical companion document explaining the WHY before the HOW. Students can read these before lab to set up the conceptual scaffolding.

R Markdown submission workflow

All problem sets and exams are submitted as R Markdown HTML outputs uploaded to Canvas. A Day-1 R Markdown tutorial walks students through install, knit, and photo-embedding.

Pre- and post-course exams

Parallel 50-point exams measure the workshop's effect on student understanding from Day 1 morning to Day 5 afternoon.

Log/logit deep dive

A dedicated guide on Day 2 with 16 worked biological examples for log and logit links — built around the most common GLM-interpretation difficulty.

JAGS & Nimble side by side

Days 4 and 5 include Nimble R scripts for the hierarchical, latent-state, and management-quantity models students will fit in the workshop.
