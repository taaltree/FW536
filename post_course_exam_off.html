FW 536 · Day 5 afternoon · 50 points

Post-course exam

Parallel to the Day-1 pre-course exam. Same topics, novel scenarios — designed to measure how much you've internalized over the five days.

Instructions

  1. Download the matching post_course_exam.Rmd template.
  2. Open it in RStudio and answer each question by adding text, code, or a photo of hand-written work.
  3. Knit to HTML and upload the .html to the Canvas Post-Course Exam assignment.
  4. Time guideline: 90 minutes (longer than pre-course because the applied case study at the end takes time).
  5. No outside collaboration. You may use the course materials, your previous problem sets, R, and your own notes.

Part A — Probability fundamentals (6 points)

A1 2 pts

Bayes' rule applied

An eDNA assay for an invasive carp has sensitivity 0.90 and specificity 0.96. The prior probability of carp presence in a randomly chosen lake is 0.10. Given a single positive eDNA sample, what is the posterior probability of presence?

A2 2 pts

Updating from two samples

Continuing A1: two independent eDNA samples from the same lake are both positive. What is the posterior probability of presence now? (Assume conditional independence of samples given presence.)

A3 2 pts

Joint probability with imperfect detection

A site has a fisher with probability 0.40 (true occupancy). Conditional on presence, each of three independent camera nights has detection probability 0.25. What is the unconditional probability of zero detections across the three nights?

Part B — Distributions (6 points)

B1 2 pts

Pick the distribution and why

Counts of salmon per redd survey have mean ≈ 5 and variance ≈ 22. Name the appropriate distribution and explain (one sentence) why it is preferred over Poisson here.

B2 2 pts

Beta-Binomial

Eight camera traps each have a per-night detection probability, but the probabilities vary across cameras. The mean detection probability is 0.05. Why does this scenario likely produce more "all-zero" deployments than a plain Binomial(8, 0.05) predicts? Sketch the underlying mechanism in 2–3 sentences.

B3 2 pts

Continuous distribution with a bound

You measure percent cover of cheatgrass at 100 plots — proportions strictly between 0 and 1, not derived from a count. Name an appropriate distribution and give one reason it fits the support of the response.

Part C — Regression and GLMs (10 points)

C1 3 pts

Log-link biology

A Poisson regression of trout counts on stream temperature gives $\hat\beta = -0.18$ per °C. Translate this into a biological statement that a manager can use, including:

  1. The multiplicative effect of 1 °C of warming on expected counts.
  2. The temperature increase that would halve expected counts.
C2 3 pts

Logit-link biology

A logistic regression of nest survival on percent canopy cover (0–100 scale) gives intercept $-3$ and slope $+0.05$ per percent canopy.

  1. What is the predicted survival probability at canopy = 0?
  2. At what canopy value does survival cross 50%?
  3. What is the marginal effect on probability of a 1-percentage-point increase in canopy, evaluated at canopy = 60?
C3 2 pts

Overdispersion diagnosis

You fit glm(y ~ x, family=poisson) and find residual deviance = 412 on 80 degrees of freedom. What does this suggest, and what is your next step?

C4 2 pts

Interaction interpretation

You fit glm(catch ~ temp * gear, family=poisson). Main effect of temp = $-0.10$; main effect of gear[Trawl] (vs. gillnet reference) = $+0.30$; interaction temp:gear[Trawl] = $+0.15$. What is the temperature effect on the trawl gear? Translate to a multiplicative effect.

Part D — Mixed models / hierarchy (8 points)

D1 2 pts

Notation

Write out, in mathematical notation, a Normal mixed-effects model with a random intercept by site for $y_{ij}$ where $j$ indexes plots within site $i$. Include the distributional assumptions on each level.

D2 3 pts

REML vs ML

Explain the rule "compare random structures with REML, compare fixed structures with ML, report REML." Why does REML give a less biased estimate of the variance components?

D3 3 pts

Random slopes

You fit a mixed model with both a random intercept and a random slope on temperature by site. The model returns estimated variances of 4.2 (intercept) and 0.05 (slope), with correlation = −0.6. Biologically, what does the negative correlation imply about how sites differ?

Part E — Maximum likelihood (6 points)

E1 3 pts

MLE by hand

You observe $n = 40$ independent Bernoulli outcomes with $k = 12$ successes. Derive the MLE of $p$ from the log-likelihood (show the steps; do not just state the answer).

E2 3 pts

LRT and profile-likelihood CI

What is a likelihood-ratio test (one sentence)? What is a profile-likelihood 95% confidence interval, and how is the "1.92" cutoff justified (one to two sentences)?

Part F — Bayes (8 points)

F1 3 pts

Conjugacy

You observe 18 successes in 30 trials. Your prior on the success probability is Beta(2, 8). Write down the posterior distribution and its mean.

F2 2 pts

BCI vs HPDI

Distinguish a Bayesian credible interval (BCI) from a highest-posterior-density interval (HPDI) in one or two sentences. When do they coincide?

F3 3 pts

Sketch a Nimble model

Sketch (just the model body, no R wrapper code) a Nimble model for $n$ observations of $y_i$ on covariate $x_i$ with a Poisson regression, log link, and vague Normal priors on the coefficients. Pseudo-code is fine; precise nimbleCode syntax is even better.

Part G — Applied case study (6 points)

G1 6 pts

From question to model

A colleague gives you the following dataset:

  • 20 streams, each visited for 5 surveys spanning 3 years.
  • Response: count of redds per survey (typical mean ≈ 4, variance ≈ 15).
  • Predictors at the survey level: water temperature, stream discharge.
  • Predictor at the stream level: a categorical management designation (3 levels).

The scientific question is: "Does the management designation affect redd abundance, after accounting for within-stream variation in temperature and discharge?"

Write a 5–8 sentence response covering:

  1. Which distribution and link function you would use, and why.
  2. How you would handle the grouping structure (streams, years).
  3. One specific way you would assess whether the model fits adequately.
  4. Whether you would prefer a frequentist or Bayesian approach for this question, and why.

There is no single right answer — credit is for clarity of reasoning and appropriate matching of method to question.
